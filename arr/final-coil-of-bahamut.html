Main Focus: Managing boss abilities and positioning
  • Cleave: Imdugud's auto-attacks cleave. Main tank should face the boss away from the group.
  • Soak: All players must stack together to share the damage.
  • Wild Charge: Targets a random player with Prey. The marked player must run behind the off-tank, as Imdugud will charge at them.
  • Electrocharge: Marks random players. They must spread out to avoid overlapping AoE damage.
  • Charge and Cyclonic Chaos: Imdugud charges to the edge and creates a tornado. Players should move to the center of the arena.
  • Celestial Rain: Large pillars appear. Players must run away as soon as they appear.
  • Critical Rip: A powerful single-target attack. Tanks should use cooldowns and healers be prepared for heavy damage.
  • Electric Burst: AoE damage to all players. Healers should be ready with group heals.
  • Spike Flail: Hits the main tank for moderate damage. Use defensive cooldowns as needed.
  • Charged Arena: The arena generates static electricity. Positioning becomes more critical to avoid overlapping AoEs.
  • Wild Charge and Electrocharge become more frequent.

Adds:

  • Son of Imdugud: Must be tanked and killed on the edges placing a voidzone by death. Applies a Vulnerability Up debuff.
  • Daughter of Imdugud: Casts Hatch, which must be interrupted and focussed by DPS.

Final Tips:

  • Wild Charge: The player with Prey must run behind the off-tank while others stay clear.
  • Tank awareness is crucial during Charge and Electrocharge.
  • Heat Lightning: Stack together to share damage.
  • Celestial Rain: Stay away from pillars to avoid damage.
  • During the add phase, prioritize killing the Daughter of Imdugud while keeping the Son of Imdugud controlled.
  • Healers should be prepared for increased damage in the second phase.

Main Focus: Managing Boss abilities and Positioning

  • Cleave: Hits two Tanks stacked together in the front, sharing the damage. DPS and Healers should stay behind the boss.
  • Nerve Gas: A ground AoE filling the arena in three patterns. Stay behind Kaliya and dodge left or right depending on the pattern.
  • Barofield: A damaging field under Kaliya for the entire fight. Stepping in applies Vulnerability Up and Heavy. Avoid it at all costs.
  • Soak: Targets a random player with a red vertical beam. The group must stack to share damage.
  • Spread: Targets two players with missiles. Players must spread to avoid group-wide damage.
  • Tank Swap: Applies Vulnerability Up and stuns the main Tank. The off-Tank must swap to take control immediately, as the next attack deals high damage.
  • Adds: Adds in the arena with specific mechanics. Tanks must keep the ALL Nodes apart to avoid a wipe.
  • Weapons Node: Takes 50% less magic damage and casts Repelling Cannons, which must be split by stacking behind it.
  • Gravity Node: Takes 50% less physical damage. It spawns Void Zones under players, avoid these zones.
  • Electric Node: Moves independently. The two Tanks need to move with it, while keeping them apart. It uses Forked Lightning. Players should spread to avoid paralysis from this mechanic. The Egg is invulnerable until the cube and sphere are dead.
  • Energy Field: Only players in melee range can damage the Electric Node and Kaliya in phase 3. Ranged DPS must move into melee range to attack.
  • Tether: Links two sets of players together. Stay close to your partner and avoid crossing the paths of the other tethered group.
  • Tether Variation: Always starts with a cone directed at a random player. Both tether groups must dodge in the same direction to avoid splitting up.

Final Tips:

  • Cleave: Tanks should stack in front of the boss, while DPS and Healers stay behind.
  • Soak and Spread: Remember to stack for Soak and spread for Spread.
  • Tank Swap: Be ready to swap quickly when Vulnerability Up is applied.
  • Adds: Keep the ALL Nodes separated and handle their specific mechanics.
  • Tether: Stay close to your tether partner and avoid crossing paths with the other group.
  • Nerve Gas: Pay attention to the pattern and dodge accordingly.
  • Barofield: Avoid stepping into it at all costs to prevent Vulnerability Up and Heavy.
  • Energy Field: Ranged DPS must move into melee range during the final phase.

Main Focus: Managing fire mechanics and add phases
  • Fountain of Fire: Marked players must stand alone in the fire, while others avoid it.
  • Tether/Pair: Paired players must stay together to avoid damage.
  • Bennu Add Phase: The Tanks must spread out and separate the Adds before killing them.
  • Revelation: A Tankbuster; Tanks must use cooldowns, and healers must be ready to heal.
  • Flames of Unforgiveness: A Raid-wide AoE that hits everyone. Healers should prepare AoE heals.
  • DPS Check: Phoenix revives with full HP. DPS must push damage to skip this mechanic.
  • Spread/Isolation: Targets a random player. Move away from the group to avoid spreading damage.
  • Point-Blank AoE: Redfire deals damage around Phoenix. Move to max melee range to avoid.
  • ADD (Interupts): Bennu Small adds that must be killed quickly. They cast Eternal Flame which must be interrupted.
  • ADD (Off-Tank Control): Blackfire add that appears. Off-tank should pick it up and face it away from the group.
  • Fountain of Fire continues during this phase. Marked players must still stand in the fire alone. Other Players must spread.

Final Tips:

  • Fountain of Fire: Only marked players should stand in the fire. Others must avoid it.
  • Tether/Pair: Stay close to your partner to avoid damage.
  • Tanks should use cooldowns for Revelation and coordinate swaps.
  • Prioritize killing Bennu adds quickly in the add phase.
  • DPS should focus on pushing damage to potentially skip the Rebirth phase.
  • Healers need to be prepared for consistent AoE damage throughout the fight.

Main Focus: Complex mechanics and careful positioning
  • Stack & Soak: Everyone must stack together to share the damage and facilitate healing.
  • Constant AoE: Continuous raid-wide damage. Healers need to keep up with healing.
  • Linear AoEs: Two marked players must run to opposite edges of the arena to safely place the AoEs.
  • Divebombs: Gather in the safe zone to avoid being knocked into the wall.
  • Tank Buster: A strong attack that requires an immediate Tank swap.
  • Raid-wide Ultimate: All Players muststack together to survive the Gigaflare.
  • Multi-mechanic Attack: Complex mechanic with multiple components:
    • Spread markers: Players must spread out to avoid overlapping damage.
    • Stack markers: Marked players must group up to share damage.
    • Towers: Players must stand in the towers to prevent massive damage.
  • Multi-hit Tank Buster: Multiple hit tank buster. Tanks must use cooldowns and stack together to share damage.
  • Enrage: Raid-wide damage that increases over time. DPS must push hard to beat the enrage.
  • Add Phase: Adds appear and must be killed quickly:
    • Large Add: Tanked by off-tank, deals heavy damage.
    • Caster Add: Casts damaging abilities, should be interrupted and killed quickly.
    • Tethered Add: Tethers to a player, must be killed before reaching them.
  • Mechanic Combination: Combination of previous mechanics:
    • Spread: Spread out to avoid overlapping damage.
    • Linear AoEs: Marked players run to edges.
    • Divebomb: Gather in safe spot after Linear AoEs resolve.

Final Tips:

  • Tanks: Coordinate cooldowns for Tank Buster and Multi-hit Tank Buster. Be ready for quick tank swaps.
  • Healers: Manage MP carefully for constant AoE healing. Coordinate cooldowns for Stack & Soak and Raid-wide Ultimate.
  • DPS: Focus on add kill priority during Add Phase. Push hard during the final phase to beat the Enrage.
  • ALL Players: Practice Multi-mechanic Attack positions and quickly identifying safe spots for Divebombs.
  • During Gigaflare, ensure everyone is stacking together to survive.
  • Communicate and call out mechanics, especially for Linear AoEs and Multi-mechanic Attack positions.
